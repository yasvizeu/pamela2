Text + Dialogue

At Pedro’s School

Marina is Brazilian and Oliver is British. They are at the airport. Marina is tired because she is traveling for work, but she is happy to practice English. Oliver is waiting for his flight and he is drinking coffee.

They are talking about their routines. Marina wakes up early, brushes her teeth, takes a shower and goes to work. Oliver says he loves Brazilian coffee and he would like to visit Rio someday.

Oliver: Hi. Are you waiting for your flight too?
Marina: Yes, I am. I am a little tired today.
Oliver: I understand. I am drinking coffee because I need energy.
Marina: I love coffee too. I am from Brazil.
Oliver: Really? That’s nice. I am from London.
Marina: Nice to meet you. What is your daily routine like?
Oliver: I wake up early, brush my teeth, take a shower and go to work.
Marina: My routine is similar. I am always busy.
Oliver: Are you traveling for work?
Marina: Yes, I am. But I am excited too.
Pamela: Hi, good morning. My name is Pamela. I am Pedro’s mom.
Secretary: Good morning. How can I help you?
Pamela: I am here because I am concerned about a message from the school.
Secretary: Of course. Let me check. How old is Pedro?
Pamela: He is four years old. He is my son.
Secretary: Is Pedro okay?
Pamela: I think so. Is the situation serious?
Secretary: No, it isn’t serious. Everything is under control.
Pamela: Thank you. I am here and I am ready to talk.
Secretary: The principal is coming shortly. She is available now.
